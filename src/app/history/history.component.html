History of Switzerland

The Founding of Switzerland

Switzerland was founded in 1291 when the three original cantons, Uri, Schwyz, and Nidwalden, signed a defensive pact against external threats. This agreement marked the beginning of the Confederation and laid the groundwork for the later political unity of Switzerland.

The Reformation

In the 16th century, Switzerland experienced a religious upheaval during the Reformation, led by figures such as Huldrych Zwingli in Zurich and John Calvin in Geneva. This movement resulted in a profound transformation of the religious landscape and a division between Reformed and Catholic regions.

World War II

During World War II, Switzerland remained neutral and was an important location for diplomatic negotiations. Despite the threat posed by the Axis powers, Switzerland managed to maintain its independence and became a safe haven for many refugees.

Modern Switzerland

In the post-war period, Switzerland developed into a significant international financial center and a leader in innovation and technology. Today, Switzerland is known for its high quality of life, neutrality, and its role as a host for international organizations such as the UN and the Red Cross.
